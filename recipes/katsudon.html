Katsudon

Katsudon" is a popular Japanese dish consisting of a breaded and deep-fried pork cutlet (tonkatsu)
served over a bowl of rice with a sweet and savory sauce, along with eggs and onions.
There's no direct connection between katsudon and a "foot" concept in traditional Japanese cuisine or culture.

Ingredients

  • Cooked rice
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 pork cutlet
  • 35g of flour
  • 50g of panko
  • 1/4-1/2 onion
  • 2 tbsp of soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp of mirin
  • 1 pinch of sugar

Steps

  1. Put 2cm of oil in a pan and heat it until 180°.

  2. In the meantime, place the cutlet rinced & dried on a cutting board
    and spread it a little by gently beating it with a rolling pit. Cut the side
    of the meat every 1.5cm. Seasonthe meat with salt and pepper.

  3. Put the meat in the beaten eggs and in the panko.

  4. Fry the cutlet (max 5min)

  5. (Before dressing) Pre cut the tonkatsu so it is easier to eat.

  6. In a frying pan, stir fry the onion, then deglace with soy sauce, mirin
    and sugar. There should be some “juice” at the bottom on the pan.

  7. Lower the heat and pour the beaten egg on top of the onions, and briefly cover.
    Cook the eggs as you like them.

  8. In a plate, place the cooked rice,
    then the onions and the eggs and on the tonkatsu on top.
